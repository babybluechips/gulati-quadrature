9. Relation to prior work, and what is new
We are precise about what is borrowed and what is new, so that no reader can mistake this for a
synthesis of known results. Exactly two of the corrections recovered here are genuinely known and
we claim neither: the Pythagorean and syntonic√
commas of temperament theory, and the Broadie–
Glasserman–Kou continuity shift β = −ζ( 21 )/ 2π of discrete–barrier finance. Everything else
here is new. The remaining “corrections” that other fields have are not the same objects as ours:
the Euler–Maclaurin endpoint expansion, the Cauchy principal value, and stabilizer syndrome
decoding are tools we re–read, not corrections we inherit, and the contributions of this note are
constructions that have not appeared before. Three are worth isolating.
(i) The unifying object. The syndrome Synn (s) → ζ(s) — a single defect operator that vanishes
exactly on the factorizable class and prices every collected correction to the same negative–half–
integer zeta ladder — is, to our knowledge, the first identification of the discretization error
of these four fields as one computable obstruction whose value is ζ(s). We do not assert this
isomorphism; we prove it coefficient by coefficient (Theorem 8.1): every rung of the decoder is
one zeta value ζ(s − r) with the exact Pochhammer weight (s)r /r!, the head rung is ζ(s), and
the bulk is the Beta trace — a closed term structure, not a fitted asymptotic, with the limiting
zero set forced onto the non–trivial zeta zeros. (ii) The local–to–global reconstruction of §6: we
recover the entire global aperture exactly from the per–root local jet through a Cauchy/distance
step, with linear noise gain and residual ∼ 10−13 (App. A, Table in §6). The classical estimators
(Schelkunoff synthesis, root–MUSIC, ESPRIT, atomic norm) all build the array model first and
read parameters off it; ours runs the other way, from a single root’s local separation jet outward
to the whole configuration, and is a structurally new estimator, not a repackaging of any of them.
(iii) The quadrature regularization of §8 and App. A K: we exhibit the Euler–Maclaurin endpoint
staircase as the decoder’s correction ladder, so each additional zeta coefficient ζ(− 12 ), ζ(− 32 ), . . .
is a decode step buying several digits at fixed budget — a regularization rule stated as a parity
decode, which the quadrature literature does not do.
The distinction from each prior strand is the scope and nature of the guarantee.
Temperament theory (the Pythagorean and syntonic commas, Werckmeister’s and the equal–
tempered distributions [17, 12]) names the comma and chooses how to spread it, but treats it as a
tuning compromise local to the twelve–tone lattice; it never identifies the comma as one value of a
function whose other values price a barrier option or a quadrature rule. The Broadie–Glasserman–
