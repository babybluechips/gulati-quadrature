Abstract
This is a pedagogical companion to the operator-spectral framework for exotic-option pricing.
The thesis is simple: when one pulls a derivative contract back to the unit circle, the pieces of
the Greek alphabet that quants memorize  theta, gamma, vega, rho  become elementary geometric objects (angular velocity, boundary curvature, normal derivative, radial position), and the
discretization corrections that desks calibrate empirically  BGK barrier shifts, Asian-averaging
biases, autocallable pencil weights  become evaluations of one EulerMaclaurin series whose coecients are Bernoulli numbers and zeta values. We work through ve contracts (vanilla call, Asian call,
discretely-monitored barrier, American put, autocallable note) using only a compass, a straightedge,
a protractor, and a nancial calculator with the О“, B , and О¶ keys. Every number can be checked on
the back of an envelope. Every picture can be drawn at a kitchen table. The framework underneath
is the branch-free Hessian of the circle divisor and its descendants; we cite the structure but reprove
the arithmetic on each example. The unifying theorem (Вҹ10) is that all four corrections are projecвҲҡ
tions of a single object: the discrete-observation renormalization of the killed semigroup by a h
boundary Hamiltonian whose smooth normal form is the Dirichlet-to-Neumann map, whose circle
normal form is the chord-distance stiness Q, whose polygon spectrum is ПҖ 2 k(N вҲ’ k)/N , and whose
corner renement is a non-diagonal Mellin matrix.

Closing remarks

The framework presented here is not an alternative to BlackScholes or to the Gaussian copula; it is a

decoder ring for them. Every standard formula in derivative pricing is, on the disk, either:

ВҲ the value of a harmonic extension at an interior point (linear pricing, NPV, Asian average);
ВҲ the value of a harmonic extension matched to a boundary obstacle (American exercise);
ВҲ a Fourier-mode contraction or expansion (Asian averaging, monitoring corrections);

ВҲ a pencil decomposition into discrete observation-bucket weights ПҖk (autocallable, and by analogy
any contract with a nite schedule of discrete exercise or knock-out tests).
